## The brief

A product hero film borrows its grammar from Apple product films: deep
negative space, controlled light, minimal copy on screen, nothing in a
hurry. The risk is copying the *look* without the *discipline* — the look
is the easy part.

The brief is to apply the discipline honestly. One product, one minute,
no rush.

## The approach

Every frame has a single focal point, and the empty space around it is
treated as composition, not as something to fill.

- The product arrives on screen, is held long enough to be understood,
  then the cut moves. No spin, no bounce, no scale-pop.
- Light is controlled and consistent — the same key from shot to shot, so
  the piece reads as one object, not a gallery of takes.
- Sound is diegetic and sparse: the click, the close, the texture. Music
  sits underneath and never competes.

## What shipped

A 1:02 hero film with square and vertical variants — reframed for each
placement, graded once, consistent across all three.
