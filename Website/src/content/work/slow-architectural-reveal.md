## The brief

Premium property rarely needs selling. It needs **showing** — at the pace
a person would actually walk the space. Most listing video does the
opposite: fast cuts, drone swoops, a music bed engineered for urgency.

This piece is built to feel like the building. Quiet, considered,
confident enough to hold still.

## The approach

The edit is paced to the architecture. Each shot holds long enough to
read the room — the light, the proportion, the materials — before moving
on. There is no narration; ambient sound does the work.

- One slow push through the main room, used once. It is the only camera
  move that draws attention to itself, so it earns its place.
- The grade keeps the interior light honest — warm where the building is
  warm, never pushed into a colour it does not have.
- Cuts are straight. No dissolves except a single cross-fade into the
  exterior at dusk.

## What shipped

A 1:38 hero film and a vertical cut sized for listings and reels — the
same restraint, reframed, not re-edited.
