## The brief

The usual assumption is that social demands the loud aesthetic — fast
cuts, big captions, a hook in the first second. The brief here rejects
that assumption. The question is whether the calm aesthetic can survive
the feed without being turned up.

It can. It just has to be edited for the format, not surrendered to it.

## The approach

The cutdowns are reframed from the hero timeline, not re-edited. The
pacing and grade are identical — what changes is the crop and the length,
not the temperament.

- No auto-generated captions and no neon text. Where type is needed, it
  is Geist, two weights, calm — the same system as everything else.
- Each cutdown carries a single idea. It is not a compressed version of
  the whole film; it is one clean thought.
- The first frame is a held shot, not a hook. The restraint *is* the
  pattern interrupt in a feed full of noise.

## What shipped

Six vertical cutdowns between 15 and 45 seconds, plus square variants of
the three strongest — all on the same calm spine.
