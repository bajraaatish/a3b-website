## The brief

Most interview content is afraid of silence. The moment a subject
finishes a sentence, the cut jumps — to b-roll, to the next question, to
anything. The viewer never gets to sit with what was just said.

This piece does the opposite. The brief is a documentary cut: keep the
pauses, trust the pacing, let the conversation breathe.

## The approach

The edit starts by finding the one idea inside the interview, then cuts
everything that does not serve it — not trims, cuts. A shorter film about
one thing beats a longer film about five.

- Pauses are kept deliberately. A held beat after an answer is doing
  work; it gives the line somewhere to land.
- B-roll appears to let the viewer rest, not to cover an edit. When the
  picture cuts away, it is a choice, not a patch.
- The grade is calm and consistent. Lifted blacks, protected skin,
  nothing pushed.

## What shipped

A 3:20 documentary cut, plus two short extracts for social — pulled from
the same timeline, never sped up.
