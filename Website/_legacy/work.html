the through-line

What every piece shares.

Different clients, different footage, same editorial spine. These choices are not negotiable — they are the brand.

01

Cut on stillness

Edits land on the quiet moment, not the action. The viewer's eye is given room to rest, then moved with intent.

02

Hard cuts only

Straight cuts and the occasional cross-dissolve. No wipes, spins, zooms, or template transitions. Ever.

03

Sound decided first

Audio leads picture. Music and sound design are chosen before the cut, never bolted on after the fact.

04

One idea per scene

If a scene is doing two things, it becomes two scenes. Clarity over density, always.

05

A calm grade

Slightly desaturated, lifted blacks, pulled highlights. Skin tones protected. Never crushed, never blown out.

06

A quiet end card

The wordmark, held still, no music swell. The piece ends the way it ran — without asking for applause.

what a3b is not

The loud aesthetic is a choice. a3b makes the other one.

Sub-second cuts, stacked transitions, neon subtitles, template packs applied without discrimination. That work is optimised for the algorithm, not the viewer. It is not wrong — it is just not what comes out of a3b.
